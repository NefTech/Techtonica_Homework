Elizabeth Warren

Elizabeth Warren is one of the most influential women in politics today. She is leading a movement to implement policies that will build and improve on the structure for the United States financial sector in terms of the way it treats individuals as well as corporations.

Political Career

  • The senior United States Senator from Massachusetts
  • From November 2006 to November 2010, Warren was a member of the FDIC Advisory Committee on Economic Inclusion
  • She is a member of the National Bankruptcy Conference, an independent organization that advises the U.S. Congress on bankruptcy law.
  • An active consumer protection advocate whose scholarship led to the passing of the Dodd–Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act, signed into law by President Obama in July 2010, and then the conception and establishment of the U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau in 2011
  • Following the 2008 U.S. financial crisis, Warren served as chair of the Congressional Oversight Panel created to oversee the Troubled Asset Relief Program (TARP)
  • Served as Assistant to the President and Special Advisor to the Secretary of the Treasury for the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau under President Barack Obama
  • Became the first female Senator from Massachusetts when she won the election on November 6, 2012
  • She was assigned to the Senate Special Committee on Aging; the Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs Committee; and the Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee

Career Before Politics

  • Warren was formerly a professor of law, and taught at the University of Texas School of Law, the University of Pennsylvania Law School, and most recently at Harvard Law School
  • A prominent scholar specializing in bankruptcy law, Warren was among the most cited in the field of commercial law before starting her political career.
